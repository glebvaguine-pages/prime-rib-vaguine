Method Overview

This recipe uses the reverse sear technique for perfectly even cooking:

  1. Dry-brine 24-48 hours uncovered in the fridge
  2. Slow roast at 250¬∞F until core reaches 118¬∞F
  3. Rest 30-90 minutes while oven preheats
  4. Sear at 500¬∞F for 10-15 minutes for crust
  5. Rest 20-30 minutes, then carve and serve

Prime Rib Step-by-Step

Step 1: Dry-Brine (24-48 Hours Ahead)

  • Unwrap roast and pat very dry with paper towels
  • Trim only thick, hard surface fat; keep soft fat cap
  • Rub all over with 3 tbsp olive oil
  • Apply 6-7 tsp flaky salt evenly, coating all sides
  • Place on wire rack set over a tray, fat-side up
  • Refrigerate uncovered for 24-48 hours

Step 2: Roast Day Prep (2-3 Hours Before Cooking)

  • Remove roast from fridge and let sit on counter (brings to room temp)
  • Preheat oven to 250¬∞F (120¬∞C)
  • In roasting pan, layer aromatic bed: rosemary, thyme, bay leaves, garlic halves, lemon zest. Drizzle with olive oil.
  • Mix herb-mustard rub: 3 garlic cloves (minced), 2 rosemary sprigs (chopped), 1 tbsp whole-grain mustard, 4 tbsp olive oil
  • Rub this mixture all over the roast surface

Step 3: Slow Roast (250¬∞F / 120¬∞C)

  • Place roast bone-side down on aromatic bed
  • Insert probe thermometer into thickest part, avoiding bone
  • Roast slowly until internal temp reaches 118¬∞F (47¬∞C)
  • Expect 2.5-3.5 hours for a 4.44 kg roast
  • Check temperature every 30 minutes after first 2 hours

Step 4: First Rest & Oven Prep

  • Remove roast from 250¬∞F oven when it hits 118¬∞F
  • Tent loosely with foil
  • Increase oven temperature to 500¬∞F (260¬∞C)
  • Let roast rest on counter for 30-90 minutes while oven preheats

Step 5: Sear Crust (500¬∞F / 260¬∞C)

  • Return roast to hot 500¬∞F oven
  • Roast for 10-15 minutes until exterior is deeply browned and crispy
  • Watch closely; don't exceed 15 minutes

Step 6: Final Rest & Carving

  • Remove from oven, tent loosely again
  • Rest for 20-30 minutes (internal temp will rise 5-8¬∞F)
  • Stand roast on end, run long knife along bones to separate meat
  • Slice ¬Ω-¬æ inch thick across the grain
  • Serve with pan jus spooned over

Pan Jus

  1. After roast is removed, place roasting pan on stovetop over medium heat
  2. Skim excess fat, leaving 2-3 tbsp of fat plus all browned bits
  3. Add 1 cup beef stock and optional splash of red wine
  4. Scrape up browned bits from pan bottom with wooden spoon
  5. Simmer 3-5 minutes until slightly reduced
  6. Season with salt and pepper to taste
  7. Strain if you prefer smooth jus; keep warm

Horseradish Cream

  1. In a bowl, combine 1 cup sour cream, 3-4 tbsp prepared horseradish, 1 tsp Dijon mustard
  2. Add 1-2 tsp lemon juice or white wine vinegar
  3. Stir well
  4. Season with salt and pepper to taste
  5. Adjust horseradish and acid to your preference (more = spicier/tangier)
  6. Chill until serving

Garlic Mashed Potatoes (serves 8)

Instructions

  1. Boil: Bring a large pot of salted water to boil. Add potato chunks and cook until fork-tender, about 20-25 minutes. Drain well.
  2. Warm cream & butter: Heat cream and garlic cloves together in a small saucepan over medium-low heat until steaming (don't boil). Let garlic infuse for 5-10 minutes. Remove from heat and let sit.
  3. Mash: Return drained potatoes to pot. Add room-temperature butter and begin mashing with a ricer or masher until smooth.
  4. Add cream: Slowly add the warm cream (with garlic cloves still in) while stirring gently. Fold in sour cream or cr√®me fra√Æche.
  5. Season: Add salt and pepper. Taste and adjust. Fold in fresh chives if using.
  6. Serve: Transfer to serving bowl. Drizzle with extra melted butter if desired. Keep warm until service.

  • Infuse the cream with garlic for deeper flavor without sharp bites
  • Make ahead: Prepare early, reheat gently on stovetop with a splash more cream
